Rule of Thirds

McDonald's

mcdonalds.com

The Rule of Thirds is the idea that it is more pleasing to the eye to view content that is placed on the 'thirds' of an area as opposed to it being centered, or otherwise symmetrically placed. In application of the Rule of Thirds, you would divide the image or content into a 3x3 grid of equal proportions and ensure that the content was placed on the dividing lines. In this example, you can see how the food is placed in the bottom third and the right-most third of the image.

Fitt's Law

Indeed

indeed.com

Fitt's Law has to do with the length of time it takes a user to be able to access the functions they desire to use. It states that the more distance there is between a mouse pointer and target area, and the smaller the size of the target area, the longer it takes to access. Indeed illustrates this by having large buttons in close proximity to the search bar.

Contrast

Amazon

amazon.com

Contrast has to do with the visibility of content on the screen based on color choice. Having a dark background with dark font is a poor choice in contrast. Amazon illustrates this by choosing dark backgrounds and light-colored text to heighten readability.
